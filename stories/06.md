Outside shadows stretch, reaching for warmth, but carry with them the cool serenity of night. She puts a cup down on the table and pushes it across. The cup is... odd, to say the least. The liquid inside is steaming hot, yet the cup is cool to the touch. It's colour, too, is odd vivid green intertwining with white. Touching the mug has one suddenly seeing the woman much clearer. Not her physical appearance, but her expressions, the small movements that tell more of her emotional state than her sweet smile lets on. There is kindness and love there, but just underneath, there is a type sadness. Deeply rooted. Not sadness, that would be the wrong word. It would be more akin to sorrow. Longing. There was something this girl had lost. "Are you almost done?", she asks, then looks at the cup. "Ah, you were resonating. That explains your spaced out expression. Will you come along? They are already waiting for us."
